About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Philosophy Department at Lone Star College--University Park. I received my PhD from the University of Notre Dame in August 2020. My research focuses on logic, the philosophy of logic, and the philosophy of mathematics. Much of my current work philosophically examines notions of translation and reduction from formal logic (especially contemporary model theory). I also have serious research interests in the philosophy of science and the history of 19th and 20th century philosophy (both Continental and Analytic traditions). Outside of philosophy, I am passionate about prison education and translating Vietnamese poetry.

My current projects include papers on:

  • A model-theoretic approach to logicism.
  • Bi-interpretability as a measure of theoretical equivalence.
  • Hermann Weyl's influence on Heidegger's philosophy.
  • Jean Cavaillès' philosophy of mathematics.
  • Tran Duc Thao's critique of Husserlian phenomenology.
